Wheel of wish

A New York banker Shanshank Chaudhary is accused of raping Roohani, who runs an NGO in Joda, Orrisa, and killing of two other men. He is beaten by the public and now he is under arrest. All the evidence is against him and everybody thinks that there is no chance of him escaping. On the other side, the book explores the creation story of Sun temple, where the king Narasingha Dev Who is a dedicated admirer of architect wants to build something unique. The main purpose of the Sun temple is to cure leprosy. 1200 people and Shilpi were summoned to build this temple in the span of 12 years. They used mathematics, astrological and scientific rules to build this temple. However, they are unable to place the magnet in the center of the temple, which is the crust of whole structure. Present day, Advocate Siddharth Rai has been asked to fight a case of Shashank. The defense lawyer is powerful and Siddharth has no record of winning. However, as time goes, he presented some eye-opening twist and turns in this case. Now it is clear that it is not a normal case.
Then the book goes on to narrate the story of the establishment of Jagannath temple in Puri. King Indradyumna has a dream to find Vishnu and he gave Vidyapati this job. They heard that one tribal man worships blue mountain and with various plans, they reached to the blue mountain and find the wooden log which is needed to further carved. However, the carving remains unfinished due to some reason. With this, the story simultaneously moves on to tell about Shashank and Roohani and their love story. All these stories are interlinked to each other and there is one conspiracy which was planned 800 years ago. So what is it? How is it taking place in present day? Who is the mastermind? Will they able to execute it accordingly? After giving answers to these questions, this book ends on an interesting note. Writing style: The narration is seamless and the link is not broken anywhere. Language is also easy and you can rapidly read this book and it will take maximum three sittings to finish this book.
